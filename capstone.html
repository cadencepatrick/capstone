Riva Lehrer

Born 1958 in Cincinatti, Ohio

  • Born with spina bifida, a spinal birth defect, Lehrer's work is influenced by the concepts of physical identity and disability.
  • One of her shows, "The Monster Studio," showcases her garment work that aims to open up conversations about stigma, particularly about people's bodies.
  • She has received numerous awards and has had her work showcased in over a dozen musems and galleries.
  • “I find the idea and study of monsters very liberating. It’s a great way to unpack and understand my own experience through a very different lens.” –Riva Lehrer

Eşref Armağan

Born 1953 in Istanbul, Türkiye

  • Born blind, Armağan taught himself how to write and oil paint with his fingers. He works in total silence, using a braille stylus to sketch outlines of his drawings. He "sees" with his fingers--he understands the shape of an object by feeling it, and translates that feeling to the canvas.
  • He participated in a 2004 University of Toronto study by John M. Kennedy that proved a person who was born blind can develop normally without seeing the outside world.
  • Armağan has had his work displayed in Türkiye, China, Italy, Czechia, and the Netherlands at over 20 exhibitions.
  • "I exist, I am living on this Earth, I am experiencing its beauties through four senses. Why should I leave without seeing it?" –Eşref Armağan

Michael Naranjo

Born 1944 in Santa Clara Pueblo, New Mexico

  • A member of the Tewa Native American tribe, Naranjo was blinded by a grenade that also maimed his right hand during the Vietnam War. Afterwards, he found a passion for clay sculpture. He has since used a variety of mediums in his work, including wax, papier-mache, bronze, and stone.
  • His mother, Rose, was a potter and sparked his initial interest in scultping. His work depicts memories from his time in the war, hunting, and other cultural and personal experiences.
  • Naranjo has won numerous awards, and his work is displayed in permanent collections in The White House and The Vatican.
  • "Being blind makes the pace of life slower; you can’t walk fast or grab for things quickly. Life is more gentle and soft and the change brought my creative energies out." –Michael Naranjo
